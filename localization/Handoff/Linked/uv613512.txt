	<title>appFigures content pack for Power BI</title>
	
	<div><p style="margin: 0in 0in 8pt;"><font face="Calibri" size="3">Tracking important statistics about your apps is easy with
Power BI and the appFigures content pack. Power BI retrieves your data, including app sales, downloads, and ad statistics, then builds a default dashboard and related reports based on that data.</font></p><p style="margin: 0in 0in 8pt;"><font face="Calibri" size="3">Connect to the <a href="https://app.powerbi.com/getdata/services/appfigures">appFigures content pack</a> for Power BI.</font></p><p style="margin: 0in 0in 8pt;"><font face="Calibri" size="3"><span><img width="1049" height="898" style="width: 558px; height: 470px;" src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/81504852/appFiguresDoc_01.png"></span></font></p><p style="margin: 0in 0in 8pt;"><br></p><font face="Calibri" size="3"><p style="margin: 0in 0in 8pt;">After you have created the connection, you can explore your
data and customize the dashboard to meet your needs. Data is&nbsp;<span style="line-height: 22.39px; font-family: Calibri; font-size: medium; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal;">automatically</span><span style="line-height: 22.39px; font-family: Calibri; font-size: medium; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal;">&nbsp;</span><span style="line-height: 1.4em;">refreshed every day.</span></p></font><font face="Calibri" size="3"><p style="margin: 0in 0in 8pt;"><br></p><strong style='font-family: "Times New Roman";'>

</strong><font face="Calibri" style='font-family: "Times New Roman";'>1.&nbsp;</font>Select <b>Get Data</b> at the bottom of the left navigation pane.<br></font><br><img style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: rgb(249, 250, 251);" src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/82642324/PBI_GetData.png"><br><br><font size="3"><p style="margin: 0in 0in 8pt; font-family: Calibri;"><font face="Calibri" style='font-family: "Times New Roman";'>2.&nbsp;</font>In the <b>Services </b>box, select <b>Get</b>.</p><p style="margin: 0in 0in 8pt; font-family: Calibri;"><span><img src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/82722055/PBI_GetServices.png"></span><br></p><p style='margin: 0in 0in 8pt; font-family: "Times New Roman";'><font face="Calibri">3. Select&nbsp;<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">appFigures</b>
&gt; &nbsp;<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal;">Connect</b>.</font></p><p style='margin: 0in 0in 8pt; font-family: "Times New Roman";'><span><img src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/82717069/appFigures.png"></span></p><p style='margin: 0in 0in 8pt; font-family: "Times New Roman";'><span style="line-height: 22.39px; font-family: Calibri; font-size: medium; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal;">3. For <b>Authentication Method</b>, select <b>oAuth2 </b>&gt; <b>Sign In</b>.</span><span><br></span></p><p style="margin: 0in 0in 8pt;"><span style="line-height: 22.39px; font-family: Calibri; font-size: medium; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal;">4.&nbsp;</span><font face="Calibri"><span style="line-height: 22.39px;">When prompted, enter your appFigures credentials and follow the appFigures authentication process.</span></font></p><p style='margin: 0in 0in 8pt; font-family: "Times New Roman";'><span style="line-height: 1.4em; font-family: Calibri;">The first time you connect, Power BI prompts you to allow read-only access to your account. Select </span><b style="font-family: Calibri;">Allow </b><span style="line-height: 1.4em; font-family: Calibri;">to begin the import process. This
can take a few minutes depending on the volume of data in your account.</span><br></p><p style='margin: 0in 0in 8pt; font-family: "Times New Roman";'>

<span><img src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/81508740/appFiguresDoc_06.png"></span></p><p style='margin: 0in 0in 8pt; font-family: "Times New Roman";'><font face="Calibri">5. After Power BI imports the data
you will see a new dashboard, report, and dataset in the left navigation pane.
New items are marked with a yellow asterisk *:</font></p><p style='margin: 0in 0in 8pt; font-family: "Times New Roman";'><span><img src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/82721875/PBI_appFigures%203.png"></span></p><p style="margin: 0in 0in 8pt; font-family: Calibri;"></p><font face="Times New Roman" style="font-family: Calibri;"><p style="margin: 0in 0in 8pt;">

<font face="Calibri">6. Select the appFigures dashboard.
This is the default dashboard that Power BI creates to display your data. You
can modify this dashboard to display your data in any way you want.</font></p><p style="margin: 0in 0in 8pt;"><font face="Calibri"><span><img src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/81508803/appFiguresDoc_08.png"></span></font></p><p style="margin: 0in 0in 8pt;"><br></p><font face="Calibri"><h3><font face="Times New Roman">

a question in the Q&amp;A box</font></a> at the top of the dashboard,&nbsp;such as "total downloads by product"</p><p style="margin: 0in 0in 8pt;"><span><img src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/81508959/appFiguresDoc_10.png"></span></p><font face="Times New Roman"><p style="margin: 0in 0in 8pt;">- <a href="https://support.powerbi.com/knowledgebase/articles/424878"><font color="#0563c1" face="Calibri">Change the
