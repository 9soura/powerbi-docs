<h2>Step 2: Explore your dataset</h2>
<br>Now that you have connected to data, explore to find insights. &nbsp;When you've found something you want to monitor, you can create a dashboard to keep up-to-date with changes.<strong>﻿</strong><span style="line-height: 1.4em;">﻿</span>

<ol><li><span><span style="line-height: 1.4em;"><span style="line-height: 14px; font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 14px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal;">Click the dataset image on the dashboard to explore the data you just connected to.<br></span><br><span><span><img src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/79746282/gettingstart4.png"></span><br></span><br></span></span></li><li><span><span style="line-height: 1.4em;">Click Got it to close the tip about creating your first chart.</span></span></li><li><span><span style="line-height: 1.4em;">In the Fields list on the right side of the page, select fields&nbsp;to build a visualization. &nbsp;Click the checkbox beside&nbsp;</span><b>﻿Gross Sales</b><span style="line-height: 1.4em;">﻿ and then&nbsp;</span><b>﻿Date</b><span style="line-height: 1.4em;">﻿.<br>

</li><li><span style="line-height: 1.4em;">Click the change visualization type button </span><img src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/76031335/change-viz-icon.png"><span style="line-height: 1.4em;">&nbsp;to switch to a different way of displaying your data. Try changing to a line chart by clicking the line chart option.</span></li>
